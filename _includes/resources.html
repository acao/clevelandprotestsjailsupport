Legal Observers

What is a Legal Observer?

A legal observer is a person who observes, monitors and documents law enforcement behavior towards demonstrators during protests and other actions. Legal observers may serve as witnesses to law enforcement misconduct or serve as a deterrent to unconstitutional behavior by law enforcement.

Do I need to be a lawyer to become a Legal Observer?

Not at all - people of wide ranging abilities and skill sets can do this work. The legal observer program is open to everyone willing to be trained and directed by NLG attorneys. Except those working for or with government or law enforcement agencies.

Where do I sign up for a training?

Please fill out the volunteer form above and check the appropriate box, and we will notify you of upcoming legal observer trainings. You can also follow The Ohio Chapter of the NLG on facebook to learn of upcoming Legal Observer trainings.

How can I learn more now?

Please read the following Legal Observer training manual. However, you will need to attend a full training and complete an application and confidentiality agreement before you are able to don the lime green NLG Legal Observer hat!
