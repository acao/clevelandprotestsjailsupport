Legal Observers

What is a Legal Observer?

A legal observer is a person who observes law enforcment activities during protests, actions, etc. and documents information that can be used to support and defend potential arrestees and to hold police accountable for their actions.

Do I need to be a lawyer to become one?

Not at all - people of wide ranging abilities and skillsets can do this work. All that is necessary is the proper training to get started.

Where do I sign up for a training?

Please fill out the volunteer form above and check the appropriate box, and we will notify you of upcoming legal observer trainings. You can also follow The Ohio Chapter of the NLG on facebook to learn of upcoming Legal Observer trainings.
